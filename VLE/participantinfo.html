Participant Information

Below you will find all the information regarding this research. You will have been supplied a copy before the researched commenced, but a copy is here for reference.

Study Title

A study into the impact of Virtual Learning Environments on first-year Coventry University studentâ€™s programming knowledge and ability.

What is the purpose of the study?

The purpose of this study is to collect data for my final year project/dissertation. This study focuses on the learning of programming by first years using different tools and environments, such as Moodle, Codecademy and Codio. There is a heavy emphasis upon self-guided learning (learning outside of timetabled sessions). The aim of the project is to see if certain environments can improve and enhance students learning of programming.

Why have I been chosen?

You have been chosen since you fit the criteria of this study, which requires first year students on programming-related courses.

Do I have to take part?

Participation in the study is entirely up to you. It is advised that you read this sheet carefully and thoroughly, and ensure you ask any questions you may have. If you agree to participate, you will be required to sign a consent form. Furthermore, you are free to withdraw from the study at any point, without providing any reasons.

What do I have to do?

In this study you will need to participate over 2-3 weeks. The schedule is as follows: Small programming test and questionnaire (30mins) which will be used to measure your programming capabilities. Following this you will be assigned an environment (e.g Codecademy) which you will be required to use for a few weeks (2-3weeks). A further programming test (30mins) which will be used to measure the benefit of the environment on your programming capabilities. Survey/Feedback from you on your experience (10mins).

What are the risks associated with this project?

There are no risks to health, safety or wellbeing associated with this study. You are able to freely withdraw without providing reasons if you so wish.

What are the benefits of taking part?

I cannot promise that the study will help you personally but the information collected from the study will help increase the understanding on learning tools and environments and how they improve the learning of programming. This research could be used to make modifications to how current and future students learn to code across all levels of programming related degrees.

Withdrawal options?

You are able to freely withdraw at any point in the study without providing reasons. To withdraw, please inform me on the contact details below that you no longer wish to participate. Any data collected will be discarded.

Data protection & confidentiality

All data collected will be kept until the submission of the project and will be secure and encrypted, in compliance with data protection. Data collected will not be able to be used to identify you.

What if things go wrong? Who to complain to?

If you have any issues or problems, you can contact me at the email below. Alternatively you can contact my supervisor at (ab9160@coventry.ac.uk) if you do not wish to complain to me. This does not affect your right to participate or withdraw.

What will happen with the results of the study?

The results of the study will be used to form my dissertation on how and if learning environments improve the learning of programming at Coventry University.
